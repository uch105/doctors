The APGAR score is a quick test performed on a newborn baby at 1 minute and 5 minutes after birth.

It evaluates five criteria:

  • Appearance (skin color)
  • Pulse (heart rate)
  • Grimace (reflex irritability)
  • Activity (muscle tone)
  • Respiration (breathing effort)

Each criterion is scored on a scale of 0 to 2, with a maximum score of 10. A higher score indicates better condition.
